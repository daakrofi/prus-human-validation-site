Instructions

You are about to be presented with a series of 500 Steam discussion posts published before the named game was released. Each item contains the post title and the captured post text used in the study. Your task is to decide whether each post contains PRUS.

What is PRUS?

Pre-Release User Speculation (PRUS) is the user-to-user expression and exchange of low-commitment conjectural propositions about an unreleased product, produced under conditions, and in service of making sense, of incomplete product information.

Simply put:

PRUS occurs when users discuss uncertain ideas about an unreleased game: what it may contain, how it may perform, what might be required to access it, or what using it might be like. These ideas do not have to be firm predictions. PRUS can also appear through questions, interpretations, concerns, hopes, suggestions, and discussion of different possibilities.

How to Code Each Post:

Read the title and the full displayed post as one connected contribution to the discussion. Select PRUS when any part of the post introduces or explores an uncertain possibility about the game or the experience surrounding it. This may be expressed in one sentence or across several connected sentences. Select Not PRUS only when the post contains no uncertain possibility about the product or product experience. This includes posts that are entirely factual, provide resolved technical information, express ordinary excitement or criticism, or discuss something unrelated to the game. The entire post does not have to be speculative. One relevant sentence, passage, or combination of statements is enough. For a PRUS post, select every product topic domain that applies to the relevant passage or passages.

Questions, requests, hopes, and suggestions

These forms of discussion can be PRUS, but they are not automatically PRUS. Read the question together with the title and surrounding post.

PRUS

A question that raises a possible feature or product state.

“Do you think the full game will have more maps?”

PRUS

A hope that introduces a possible product feature.

“I hope they add split-screen before release.”

PRUS

A suggestion that raises an unresolved product concern.

“They should improve the servers before launch because they may struggle with the player count.”

Not PRUS

A question seeking only an immediate technical solution.

“My controller isn’t working. Which setting fixes it?”

PRUS

A current problem used to raise a wider product concern.

“Controller support is still broken this close to launch. Is the full game going to support controllers properly?”

Not PRUS

A request for a currently knowable fact.

“Which edition includes early access?”

Not PRUS

A factual instruction rather than speculation.

“Press F5 to open the settings menu.”

Select PRUS when a question raises or implies uncertainty about what the game contains, how it performs, what it requires, or what the wider product experience may be like. The question does not need to mention the future directly. Select Not PRUS when it only asks for a currently available fact, gameplay instruction, or solution to an immediate technical problem. A useful check is: would a straightforward factual answer or troubleshooting solution fully satisfy the post? If yes, it is probably Not PRUS. If the question also explores a wider uncertain possibility about the product, it may be PRUS.

Demos, betas, and Early Access

Discussion of a pre-release version can be PRUS. Users do not always state directly that a current issue may continue after release. Concern about an unresolved pre-release experience may itself suggest uncertainty about the game or its release state.

PRUS

An unresolved concern about a pre-release experience.

“The demo keeps crashing, which worries me.”

PRUS

A current experience that raises a possibility about the wider game.

“The combat feels limited in the demo. Is this all there will be?”

PRUS

A concern about how a pre-release problem may develop.

“The beta performance is rough. Hopefully they can fix it before launch.”

Not PRUS

A resolved technical problem.

“The demo crashed because my graphics driver was outdated. Updating it fixed the problem.”

A current problem does not automatically count as PRUS. It should involve an unresolved concern, interpretation, possibility, or question about the product or product experience.

Content

Speculation about story, mechanics, characters, modes, maps, features, monetization, or gameplay experience.

Example: "The demo probably hints at a bigger crafting system."

Performance

Speculation about technical quality, optimization, FPS, bugs, crashes, servers, or launch stability.

Example: "It might run badly on older GPUs at launch."

Requirements / Access

Speculation about platform access, hardware specs, storage, regions, editions, release timing, or play conditions.

Example: "The full version may need more RAM than the demo."
